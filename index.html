BIOGRAPHY

Piet Mondrian, 1872-1944, was a Dutch painter who lived in The Netherlands, Paris, London, and New York. Throughout his career, his art evolved through several distinct styles. He is remembered primarily for the style of art he invented and called "neo-plasticism."

Mondrian studied art at Rijksadame van Beeldende in Amsterdam from 1892 until 1897. During this time, his artwork was primarily Impressionistic, in keeping with The Hague School of painting, the Dutch style of the time. Over the next decade, Mondrian developed his style, abstracting his subjects more and more, before moving to Paris in 1912 and taking his work in a Cubist direction.

Mondrian returned to The Netherlands in 1914 where he founded De Stijl (The Style) with Theo van Doesburg. De Stijl was a journal and an art movement whose primary style was the neo-plasticism that Mondrian described in articles in their journal and other publications.

In 1919, Mondrian returned to Paris, now established as a major figure on the cutting edge of contemporary art. Here he continued to refine his neo-plastic style that he would be known for, write, exhibit, and act as an artistic leader of the avant-garde.
